# Non-Accelerating Inflation Rate of Unemployment (NAIRU)

## 中文解释 / English Explanation

* **定义 / Definition**  
  **中文解释**：非加速通货膨胀失业率（NAIRU）是指在不引发通货膨胀加速的情况下，经济能够维持的最低失业率水平。这一概念由经济学家米尔顿·弗里德曼和埃德蒙·菲尔普斯在20世纪60年代提出，核心思想是当失业率低于NAIRU时，劳动力市场紧张会导致工资和物价上涨，从而加速通货膨胀。  
  **English Explanation**: The Non-Accelerating Inflation Rate of Unemployment (NAIRU) refers to the lowest level of unemployment that an economy can sustain without causing inflation to accelerate. This concept was introduced by economists Milton Friedman and Edmund Phelps in the 1960s, based on the idea that when unemployment falls below NAIRU, tight labor market conditions lead to wage and price increases, thereby accelerating inflation.

* **应用 / Application**  
  **中文应用场景**：NAIRU常用于宏观经济政策制定中，特别是货币政策和财政政策的调整。例如，中央银行在设定利率时，会参考NAIRU来判断是否需要通过加息来抑制通胀风险。需要注意的是，NAIRU并非固定值，它会随着经济结构、技术进步和劳动力市场的变化而波动。  
  **English Application Scenarios**: NAIRU is often used in macroeconomic policy-making, particularly in adjusting monetary and fiscal policies. For instance, central banks refer to NAIRU when setting interest rates to determine whether rate hikes are needed to curb inflation risks. It is important to note that NAIRU is not a fixed value; it fluctuates with changes in economic structure, technological advancements, and labor market dynamics.

* **重要性 / Significance**  
  **中文重要性**：从监管角度看，NAIRU为政策制定者提供了重要的参考指标，帮助平衡就业与通胀目标；从投资角度看，理解NAIRU有助于预测利率走势和经济周期；从行业角度看，企业可根据NAIRU调整用工策略以应对成本压力。  
  **English Significance**: From a regulatory perspective, NAIRU provides policymakers with a crucial reference for balancing employment and inflation targets. From an investment standpoint, understanding NAIRU helps predict interest rate trends and economic cycles. From an industry perspective, businesses can adjust their labor strategies based on NAIRU to manage cost pressures.

## 历史典故 / Historical Context

* **起源 / Origin**  
  **中文起源**：20世纪60年代，米尔顿·弗里德曼和埃德蒙·菲尔普斯分别独立提出了“自然失业率”理论，后发展为NAIRU概念。这一理论挑战了当时盛行的菲利普斯曲线观点（即失业率与通胀存在稳定的负相关关系），强调长期来看通胀预期会抵消低失业率带来的通胀压力。  
  **English Origin**: In the 1960s, Milton Friedman and Edmund Phelps independently proposed the "natural rate of unemployment" theory, which later evolved into the concept of NAIRU. This theory challenged the prevailing Phillips Curve view (which posited a stable inverse relationship between unemployment and inflation), emphasizing that in the long run, inflation expectations would offset inflationary pressures caused by low unemployment.

* **影响 / Impact**  
  **中文影响**：NAIRU的提出深刻影响了宏观经济理论和政策实践。它帮助解释了20世纪70年代的“滞胀”现象（高通胀与高失业并存），并为现代货币政策框架奠定了基础。目前，许多国家的中央银行仍将NAIRU作为制定政策的重要依据之一。  
  **English Impact**: The introduction of NAIRu profoundly influenced macroeconomic theory and policy practice. It helped explain the "stagflation" phenomenon of the 1970s (high inflation coupled with high unemployment) and laid the foundation for modern monetary policy frameworks. Today, many central banks still use NAIru as a key reference for policy-making.
