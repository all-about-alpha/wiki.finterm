# Rollover

## 中文解释 / English Explanation

* **定义 / Definition**  
  **中文解释**: 展期（Rollover）是指将即将到期的金融工具（如贷款、债券或期权）延长至新的到期日的过程。这一操作通常涉及重新协商条款或支付一定的费用，以避免立即偿还本金或执行合约。展期常见于外汇交易、贷款续借和退休账户转移等场景。  
  **English Explanation**: A rollover refers to the process of extending the maturity date of a financial instrument (such as a loan, bond, or option) that is nearing its expiration. This operation typically involves renegotiating terms or paying a fee to avoid immediate repayment of principal or contract execution. Rollovers are commonly seen in forex trading, loan renewals, and retirement account transfers.

* **应用 / Application**  
  **中文应用**: 展期广泛应用于外汇交易中，投资者通过展期将未平仓头寸延至下一个交易日，以避免结算风险。在贷款领域，借款人可通过展期延长还款期限，缓解短期资金压力。然而，展期可能带来额外成本或利率风险，需谨慎评估。  
  **English Application**: Rollovers are widely used in forex trading, where investors extend open positions to the next trading day to avoid settlement risks. In the lending sector, borrowers can extend repayment periods through rollovers to alleviate short-term financial pressure. However, rollovers may incur additional costs or interest rate risks and require careful evaluation.

* **重要性 / Significance**  
  **中文重要性**: 从监管角度看，展期机制有助于维护市场流动性，防止因集中到期导致的系统性风险。从投资角度，它为投资者提供了灵活管理头寸的工具。在行业层面，展期是金融市场不可或缺的操作之一，尤其在衍生品和外汇市场中至关重要。  
  **English Significance**: From a regulatory perspective, rollover mechanisms help maintain market liquidity and prevent systemic risks caused by concentrated maturities. From an investment standpoint, it provides investors with tools for flexible position management. At the industry level, rollovers are an indispensable operation in financial markets, especially in derivatives and forex markets.

## 历史典故 / Historical Context

* **起源 / Origin**  
  **中文起源**: 展期的概念最早可追溯至20世纪初的外汇市场。随着全球贸易和金融活动的增加，投资者需要一种机制来管理跨时区的交易结算问题。1970年代布雷顿森林体系解体后，浮动汇率制普及，展期成为外汇交易中的标准操作之一。  
  **English Origin**: The concept of rollover dates back to the early 20th century in the forex market. With the growth of global trade and financial activities, investors needed a mechanism to manage cross-timezone transaction settlements. After the collapse of the Bretton Woods system in the 1970s and the adoption of floating exchange rates, rollovers became a standard practice in forex trading.

* **影响 / Impact**  
  **中文影响**: 展期的引入极大地提升了金融市场的灵活性和效率。它帮助企业和投资者更好地管理现金流和风险敞口。如今，展期已成为全球金融市场的基础操作之一，广泛应用于外汇、衍生品和债务管理等领域。  
  **English Impact**: The introduction of rollovers significantly enhanced the flexibility and efficiency of financial markets. It has helped businesses and investors better manage cash flow and risk exposure. Today, rollovers are a fundamental operation in global financial markets, widely applied in forex, derivatives, and debt management.

  3. 【延伸意义】:  维护市场流动性并降低系统性风险。
