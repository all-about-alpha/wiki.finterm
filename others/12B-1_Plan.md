# 12B-1 Plan

## 中文解释 / English Explanation

* **定义 / Definition**  
  **中文解释**：12B-1计划是美国证券交易委员会（SEC）根据《1940年投资公司法》第12B-1条款制定的一项规则，允许共同基金从其资产中扣除费用，用于支付分销和营销费用。这些费用通常包括广告、销售佣金、投资者服务等。12B-1费用是基金年度运营费用的一部分，通常以年化百分比的形式表示。  
  **English Explanation**: The 12B-1 Plan is a rule established by the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) under Section 12B-1 of the Investment Company Act of 1940, which permits mutual funds to deduct fees from their assets to cover distribution and marketing expenses. These fees typically include advertising, sales commissions, and investor services. The 12B-1 fee is part of a fund's annual operating expenses and is usually expressed as an annual percentage.

* **应用 / Application**  
  **中文解释**：12B-1计划主要应用于共同基金的销售和推广过程中。基金公司通过收取12B-1费用来支付销售渠道的佣金、广告费用以及投资者服务成本。投资者在选择基金时需要注意12B-1费用，因为它会直接影响基金的总费用率（TER）和长期投资回报。高12B-1费用可能意味着更高的投资成本，从而降低净收益。  
  **English Explanation**: The 12B-1 Plan is primarily used in the sales and promotion of mutual funds. Fund companies use 12B-1 fees to pay for sales channel commissions, advertising costs, and investor service expenses. Investors should be aware of 12B-1 fees when selecting funds, as they directly impact the fund's total expense ratio (TER) and long-term investment returns. High 12B-1 fees may indicate higher investment costs, thereby reducing net returns.

* **重要性 / Significance**  
  **中文解释**：从监管角度看，12B-1计划为基金公司提供了一种合法的费用分摊机制，有助于推动基金的销售和推广。从投资者角度看，12B-1费用是评估基金成本的重要指标之一，高费用可能侵蚀投资收益。从行业角度看，12B-1计划促进了基金分销渠道的多样化，但也引发了关于费用透明度和合理性的讨论。  
  **English Explanation**: From a regulatory perspective, the 12B-1 Plan provides a legal mechanism for fund companies to share expenses, facilitating fund sales and promotion. From an investor's perspective, 12B-1 fees are a critical metric for evaluating fund costs, as high fees can erode investment returns. From an industry perspective, the 12B-1 Plan has diversified fund distribution channels but has also sparked discussions about fee transparency and fairness.

## 历史典故 / Historical Context

* **起源 / Origin**  
  **中文解释**：12B-1计划起源于1980年，当时美国证券交易委员会（SEC）为了促进共同基金行业的发展，允许基金公司通过收取额外费用来支付分销和营销成本。这一规则的制定旨在帮助基金公司扩大投资者基础，同时为投资者提供更多的投资选择。12B-1计划的实施标志着共同基金行业从传统的销售模式向市场化推广的转变。  
  **English Explanation**: The 12B-1 Plan originated in 1980 when the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) allowed fund companies to charge additional fees to cover distribution and marketing costs, aiming to promote the growth of the mutual fund industry. This rule was designed to help fund companies expand their investor base while providing investors with more investment options. The implementation of the 12B-1 Plan marked a shift in the mutual fund industry from traditional sales models to market-driven promotion.

* **影响 / Impact**  
  **中文解释**：12B-1计划对共同基金行业产生了深远影响。它推动了基金销售渠道的多样化，促进了基金市场的竞争和创新。然而，随着时间推移，12B-1费用也引发了争议，批评者认为这些费用增加了投资者的负担，且缺乏透明度。近年来，监管机构加强了对12B-1费用的审查，要求基金公司更清晰地披露费用结构。  
  **English Explanation**: The 12B-1 Plan has had a profound impact on the mutual fund industry. It has diversified fund sales channels and fostered competition and innovation in the fund market. However, over time, 12B-1 fees have sparked controversy, with critics arguing that these fees increase investor burdens and lack transparency. In recent years, regulators have intensified scrutiny of 12B-1 fees, requiring fund companies to disclose fee structures more clearly.
