# Chapter 15 Bankruptcy

## 中文解释 / English Explanation

* **定义 / Definition**  
  **中文解释**：第15章破产（Chapter 15 Bankruptcy）是美国《破产法》中的一部分，专门用于处理跨国破产案件。它基于《联合国国际贸易法委员会跨国破产示范法》（UNCITRAL Model Law on Cross-Border Insolvency），旨在为跨国破产案件提供法律框架，协调不同司法管辖区的破产程序，保护债权人和债务人的权益。  
  **English Explanation**: Chapter 15 Bankruptcy is a section of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code specifically designed to handle cross-border insolvency cases. It is based on the UNCITRAL Model Law on Cross-Border Insolvency and aims to provide a legal framework for international bankruptcy cases, coordinating insolvency proceedings across different jurisdictions to protect the rights of creditors and debtors.

* **应用 / Application**  
  **中文解释**：第15章破产适用于涉及多个国家的破产案件，例如跨国公司的破产重组或清算。它允许外国破产代表在美国法院申请承认其在本国的破产程序，从而避免重复诉讼和资源浪费。应用场景包括跨国企业的债务重组、资产清算以及债权人权益的保护。需要注意的是，第15章破产并不直接解决债务问题，而是通过国际合作促进破产程序的顺利进行。  
  **English Explanation**: Chapter 15 Bankruptcy applies to insolvency cases involving multiple countries, such as the restructuring or liquidation of multinational corporations. It allows foreign insolvency representatives to seek recognition of their home country's bankruptcy proceedings in U.S. courts, thereby avoiding duplicate litigation and resource waste. Application scenarios include debt restructuring of multinational enterprises, asset liquidation, and protection of creditor rights. It is important to note that Chapter 15 does not directly resolve debt issues but facilitates the smooth progress of bankruptcy proceedings through international cooperation.

* **重要性 / Significance**  
  **中文解释**：第15章破产在国际破产领域具有重要意义。它为跨国破产案件提供了统一的法律框架，减少了法律冲突，提高了破产程序的效率。从监管角度看，它有助于维护全球金融市场的稳定；从投资角度看，它为跨国投资者提供了更明确的法律保障；从行业角度看，它促进了跨国企业的有序退出或重组。  
  **English Explanation**: Chapter 15 Bankruptcy holds significant importance in the field of international insolvency. It provides a unified legal framework for cross-border bankruptcy cases, reduces legal conflicts, and improves the efficiency of insolvency proceedings. From a regulatory perspective, it helps maintain the stability of global financial markets; from an investment perspective, it offers clearer legal protections for international investors; and from an industry perspective, it facilitates the orderly exit or restructuring of multinational enterprises.

## 历史典故 / Historical Context

* **起源 / Origin**  
  **中文解释**：第15章破产的起源可以追溯到2005年，当时美国国会通过了《破产滥用预防及消费者保护法案》（BAPCPA），将《联合国国际贸易法委员会跨国破产示范法》纳入美国《破产法》，形成了第15章。这一举措是为了应对全球化背景下日益增多的跨国破产案件，解决不同国家法律体系之间的冲突。  
  **English Explanation**: The origin of Chapter 15 Bankruptcy dates back to 2005 when the U.S. Congress passed the Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention and Consumer Protection Act (BAPCPA), incorporating the UNCITRAL Model Law on Cross-Border Insolvency into the U.S. Bankruptcy Code as Chapter 15. This move was aimed at addressing the increasing number of cross-border insolvency cases in the context of globalization and resolving conflicts between different legal systems.

* **影响 / Impact**  
  **中文解释**：第15章破产的实施对全球破产法律体系产生了深远影响。它促进了各国在破产领域的合作，减少了法律冲突，提高了破产程序的透明度和可预测性。目前，第15章已成为处理跨国破产案件的重要工具，广泛应用于全球范围内的企业重组和清算。  
  **English Explanation**: The implementation of Chapter 15 Bankruptcy has had a profound impact on the global insolvency legal system. It has promoted international cooperation in the field of bankruptcy, reduced legal conflicts, and enhanced the transparency and predictability of insolvency proceedings. Today, Chapter 15 has become a crucial tool for handling cross-border insolvency cases and is widely used in corporate restructuring and liquidation worldwide.

## 要点总结 / Takeaway

* **中文**  
  - **核心价值**：为跨国破产案件提供统一的法律框架，促进国际合作。  
  - **应用场景**：跨国企业的债务重组、资产清算及债权人权益保护。  
  - **延伸意义**：减少法律冲突，提高破产程序的效率和透明度。

* **English**  
  - **Key Point 1**: Provides a unified legal framework for cross-border insolvency cases, promoting international cooperation.

  - **Key Point 3**: Reduces legal conflicts and enhances the efficiency and transparency of bankruptcy proceedings.
